Our Story

The story of three high school students, a flawed STEM education system and their solution.

The Problem

STEM is the future. With robotics, self-driving cars and space exploration all growing by the day, it's clear we need a new generation of innovators, think Many young students begin with an interest in technology but that interest fades away due to the lack of opportunities. School doesn't provide substantial STEM education and other programs are too expensive for students to aford. Kinet-X works with the community to provide affordable, project-based learning. We teach students to become innovators - the Kinet-X way.

What We Saw

The STEM world is growing, and everyone knows it. The question is why isn't more being done to support it's growth? Many schools aren't offering CS programs, and even less offer any kind of engineering class. Outside programs are expensive, and generally geared towards older people. How is the new generation supposed to be inspired when there's nothing to get excited about?

The Beginning

There needed to be a STEM program out there geared towards the next generation. One that didn't cost thousands of dollars. One that gave you an accurate scope of the STEM world, that taught several topics. A program taught by people that knew what it was like entering the world of tech. That was the start of Kinet-X.

What We've Done

Since then, Kinet-X has grown from being an idea and a dream, to being reality. We've met and worked with employees and heads of tech companies around the city. We've learned about what it takes to teach STEM, and seen what works and what doesn't. Over the summer of 2016, we held our first summer program; which was definitely a success. Read about what the students thought on our Testimonials Page!


What We're Doing

Our curriculum is constantly being improved. What we teach shouldn't be static, lessons are for the students so why shouldn't their input matter? We are reaching out to middle schools all over the city, to try and reach as many people as possible. Also be sure to look out for Kinet-X in the press and online, we're doing everything we can to get out! Change your future, find your passion. Sign Up Today.

Hi! My name is Adam Abbas, I'm 16 and a junior at Stuyvesant High School. I've always been attracted to the maker culture, being fascinated by anything mechanical. I started programming in the 7th grade, when I learned basic Python and Web Development. Since then, I haven't stopped learning; picking up new languages and expanding on what I already knew. I love teaching, and sharing the passion I have for coding and making something amazing from a blank, white screen. Kinet-X is just a way for me to inspire, and show the world how amazing and painless programming really is!

I'm a 16 year old junior at Stuyvesant High School, who grew up in Brooklyn. I was first introduced to the world of electronics at a very young age, as my father would constantly be tinkering or working on a project at home. The lights, the wires- it fascinated me. From the beginning, I aspired to become an engineer.My first formal introduction into electrical engineering came as a 6th grader, when I was lucky enough to to work with NASA engineers. Through this experience, I was able to grow my skillet, and watch my passion for the field grow. Engineering is such a huge and interesting field- there's so much to learn! I hope Kinet-X will allow me to share my passion with younger students, and maybe even teach me a thing or two!
