Who are we?

The Future

The Beginning

Three high school friends, all with some connection to STEM, discussing what originally got them into the STEM field. The robotics team, their father, a friend. All three were fortunate enough to have some gateway. But what about students who don't? Students growing up in poor neighborhoods and going to underfunded schools? Where do they turn to? They turn to extracurricular programs. Weekend programs, summer programs. Overpriced programs. How are you suppoed to discover STEM when you need thousands of dollars just to experience it? We decided that needed to change.

The Problem

The world needs innovators, thinkers and problem solvers. As of right now, the amount of engineering graduates is less than 10 percent of that of psychology or law graduates. There are kids who never get to experience engineering, programming or circuitry. Schools just don't offer those programs, summer programs are too expensive and only provide a basic classroom experience. We need a new kind of program. One that makes learning fun, one that inspires. We need Kinet-X.

The Solution

The idea is simple. A program that makes learning Engineering enjoyable, affordable, educational and makes an impact.

Enjoyable

It should be obvious that taking notes and staring at a slideshow isn't the best way to learn any form of technical skill- including engineering. Why is it that that's how it's being taught then? Lessons need to be hands-on. Activities have to range in difficulty, from ones that test basic understanding to ones that present a challenge far beyond the students' level, so that no one feels left behind. No one should leave a program feeling empty because they couldn't keep up, or feeling like they learned nothing because they knew it all. Student's should leave a program feeling content, and proud of what they accomplished.

Affordable

It's time that price stops being the reason students miss out on discovering their passion. Kinet-X has a base cost of $650- which includes meals/snacks and cost of materials for the entire 4-weeks. However, price is a factor that we refuse to inhibit student's from changing the future, so Kinet-X will offer need-based financial aid to those that are enthusiastic about joining our program.

Educational

Working with companies like General Assembly and Knewton, we strive to ensure our content is the best. We strive to utilise peer mentorship - fellow youth with experience in tech . The goal is for students to feel comfortable with their teachers, to not be afraid to make mistakes and ask questions. Kinet-X also provides trained volunteers - industry professionals in tech - to partner with our peer mentors and work as a team to deliver tech education.

Making an Impact

Kinet-X isn't just a summer program. It's a movement. New problems are popping up everyday, and people aren't prepared to solve them. We need to not only educate, but inspire the next generation. We need to get rid of the "push" factors that are stopping people from entering the engineering world. And it all starts in the schools. Starting in the fall, Kinet-X will be working with schools- private, charter and public- to help develop Computer Science curriculums, and ways to apply them. Kinet-X is already partnered with the Pine Street School near Wall Street. If your school is interested in partnering with us, please reach out to us at info@kinet-x.org

Some Numbers

Or as we called it, our reality check.

  • The average job growth rate is generally predicted to be 7%, but Infastructure, Biomedical and Wind Energy Engineering are growing at 21%, 27% and 108% respectively.
  • 56 Billion dollars were spent on tech education in 2015
  • Out of the 42 thousand schools in the United States, 2.1 thousand are certified to teach computer science.
  • For every qualified school, an average of 15 students takes the AP Computer Science test
